V is for Vulcan

Written by Andy Tongue

Aston Martin’s new track-only supercar RAISES LOTS OF EXPECTATIONS, but David Lillywhite ISN’T DISAPPOINTED AS HE experiences the vulcan WITH ASTON MARTIN RACING DRIVER DARREN TURNER AT THE PAUL RICARD CIRCUIT IN THE SOUTH OF FRANCE

Breakfast at the luxurious Hôtel du Castellet. The view over the terrace is of manicured lawns, tranquil pools and graceful statues while, in the distance, the sun plays over the Sainte-Baume mountains. Who would ever know, then, that the Paul Ricard motor-racing circuit, home to the French Grand Prix from 1971 to 1990, and notoriously fast and challenging, is located in this otherwise peaceful region, as well as next door to the Hôtel du Castellet? It’s here that Aston Martin has invited the first buyers of its new £1.5 million Vulcan supercar, limited to 24 models, for the first in a series of events aimed at preparing Vulcan owners to take their cars on track. The plan is this: an instructor will take each of us out in a V12 Vantage S to show us the track. Then we’ll swap places and drive the V12 until we are comfortable, before moving on to the One-77, followed by the Vantage GT4 on slick tyres. And then, from the passenger seat of the Vulcan, we’ll get to see how it’s really done by Aston Martin Racing’s Darren Turner. It’s a lot to take in, but it’s not long before the first of us are hopping into the V12 Vantage S driver’s seat.
